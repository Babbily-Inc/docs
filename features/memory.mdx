---
title: "Memory"
description: "Save context Babbily should reuse across chats—plus a profile of who you are and what you're working on."
---

Memory lets Babbily remember things between conversations. Instead of re-explaining your role, projects, or preferences every time, you tell Babbily once and it carries that context forward. Babbily also builds a lightweight **memory profile** that organizes what it knows about you into sections you can review and edit.

## When to use memory

Use memory for anything you'd want every future chat to know, such as:

- Your role, company, or team.
- Projects you're actively working on.
- Tools, frameworks, or stacks you prefer.
- Long-running preferences ("always answer in metric units," "use a concise tone").
- People, products, or accounts that come up often.

For one-off context that should only apply to the current chat, just say it in the message—you do not need to save it.

## Save something to memory

The fastest way to add a memory is to tell Babbily directly in any chat:

```text
Remember that I'm a product designer at Acme working on the Atlas redesign.
```

```text
Forget that I live in Austin.
```

Babbily confirms what was saved or removed and the new context becomes available in your next reply and every chat after it.

You can also manage memory from settings:

1. Open **Settings → Memory Profile**.
2. Use the input at the bottom to tell Babbily what to remember or forget.
3. Review existing sections (see below) and delete any item you no longer want kept.

## Your memory profile

Babbily organizes saved context into sections so it's easy to skim and edit:

- **Work context** – your role, company, team, and active projects.
- **Personal context** – preferences, locations, people, and ongoing personal threads.
- **Top of mind** – the most recently mentioned context.
- **Brief history** – recent context Babbily has been using.
- **Recent context** – older items still considered relevant.
- **Earlier context** – background items kept for occasional reference.
- **Long-term background** – stable, slow-changing facts.

Sections shift over time as Babbily learns what's current versus historical. You can delete any item to drop it from all future chats.

## Citations in replies

When Babbily uses something from your memory in a reply, it shows a citation you can hover or click to see exactly what was used. Citations come from two sources:

- **Babbily memory** – context you explicitly told Babbily to remember.
- **Supermemory** – longer-term documents and notes synced from your profile.

Click a citation to jump to that item in your memory profile, where you can edit or delete it.

## Profile fields

The fields you fill out in **Settings → Profile** (name, role, preferred tone, and so on) are synced to memory automatically when you save. Updating your profile updates what Babbily remembers about you—no extra step required.

## Delete memory

You have full control over what Babbily keeps:

- **Forget one item.** Say "forget that…" in chat, or open **Settings → Memory Profile** and delete the item.
- **Forget everything.** In **Settings → Memory Profile**, clear each section, or contact support to wipe all stored memory for your account.

Deleted items are removed from future chats immediately and do not influence new replies.
