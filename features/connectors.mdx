Connectors let Babbily reach into the tools you already use—email, files, calendars, code, customer data—and bring that context into a conversation. When a connector is on, Babbily can search, summarize, and take actions inside the linked app while you chat.

## When to use connectors

Turn on a connector when you want Babbily to:

- Reference live data from another app (for example, "summarize the threads in my inbox from Acme this week").
- Draft an action against a connected app (for example, an email reply or a calendar invite) before you send it.
- Pull project, file, or repo context into a conversation without copy-pasting.

If you only want a general chat or web search, you do not need to connect anything.

## Connect an app

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Connectors">
    Go to **Settings → Connectors**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a connector">
    Pick the app you want to link. A popup window opens to that app's sign-in flow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Babbily">
    Sign in and grant the scopes Babbily requests. The popup closes when authorization completes and the connector shows as connected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use it in chat">
    Start a new message and reference the app naturally. You can also open the composer menu and pick the connector explicitly for that turn.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Use a connector in chat

Once connected, you can:

- **Ask in plain language.** "Find the latest thread with the design team in Slack" or "What did I commit to in last week's Asana standup?"
- **Pick a connector for the turn.** Open the composer menu next to the message box and select the connector you want Babbily to prefer for that reply.
- **Run a slash command.** Type `/` in the composer to see commands for connected apps (for example, search a specific Drive folder or summarize a Notion page).

## Approve actions before they run

Some connectors can take actions, not just read data. When Babbily prepares an action—like sending an email or creating a calendar event—it pauses and shows you a draft to review.

You can:

- **Approve** to run the action as drafted.
- **Edit** the draft inline, then approve.
- **Cancel** to discard the draft without running anything.

Nothing is sent or written to a connected app until you approve it.

## Manage permissions

To review or remove access:

1. Open **Settings → Connectors**.
2. Select the connector.
3. Use **Disconnect** to revoke Babbily's access immediately. You can reconnect at any time.

Disconnecting removes the credential from Babbily but does not delete anything in the underlying app.

## Privacy

- Connector credentials are stored encrypted and used only to fulfill your requests.
- Babbily does not train models on your connected data.
- Action drafts always require your approval before they run.
