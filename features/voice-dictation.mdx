Voice dictation lets you speak instead of typing when your browser or device supports it.

## Where you can use dictation

You can use voice dictation in:

- Chat.
- Translate.

Look for the microphone button near the message box or source text field.

## Dictate text

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the microphone">
    Click the microphone button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Allow microphone access">
    If your browser or device asks for permission, allow microphone access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak clearly">
    Babbily listens and adds the transcript to the text field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review before sending">
    Check the text before you send a chat message or use a translation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for better dictation

- Use a quiet environment when possible.
- Speak in complete phrases.
- Review names, numbers, and technical terms before sending.
- In Translate, choose the source language when auto-detect is not enough.

## Troubleshooting

If dictation does not start:

- Check microphone permission for your browser or app.
- Make sure another app is not using your microphone.
- Try refreshing Babbily.
- Type or paste text if your current browser does not support voice input.

<Warning>
  Review dictated text before sending it. Microphone transcription can misunderstand names, numbers, accents, or specialized terms.
</Warning>
