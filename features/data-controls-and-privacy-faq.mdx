This FAQ explains the customer-visible controls you can use to manage what you share with Babbily.

<Warning>
  Do not send passwords, verification codes, private keys, payment card numbers, government identifiers, or confidential files you are not allowed to use in Babbily.
</Warning>

## Quick control map

| Area | Where to manage it | What you can do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Memory | **Settings > Memory** | Review your Memory Profile, remove eligible citations, or ask Babbily to forget something. |
| Connectors | **Settings > Connectors** | Connect apps, review authorization status, and manage access where controls are available. |
| Temporary Chat | Composer or chat controls | Start a chat that is not saved like normal chat history. |
| Sharing | Chat share controls | Create, review, or stop using public share links. |
| Library | **Library** | Review saved chats, translations, and media. Delete supported saved items. |
| Account | **Settings > Account** | Update account details, request password help, log out, or contact support. |

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What can I control directly?">
    You control what messages you send, what files you attach, what memory you keep, which apps you connect, which connector actions you approve, which chats you share, and which supported Library items you delete.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I review or remove memory?">
    Open **Settings > Memory** to review your Memory Profile and memory citations. You can ask Babbily to forget something, or delete eligible citations from the Memory page.

    Try:

    ```text
    Forget that I prefer long answers.
    ```

    Removed memory stops affecting future replies. If you cannot remove something from the UI, contact support.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does Temporary Chat use memory?">
    Temporary Chat is for conversations you do not want saved in normal chat history or Library. Temporary chats are not meant for sharing and do not use or update Babbily memory.

    Temporary Chat is still a work surface, so avoid entering passwords, private keys, payment details, or other secrets.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens when I connect an app?">
    Connectors let Babbily use access you authorize in the connected app. Connected apps may limit what Babbily can see or do based on your role and permissions.

    Some connected-app actions ask for approval before they run. Review sends, updates, billing-related changes, customer-data actions, and workspace changes carefully before approving.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I remove connector access?">
    Use **Settings > Connectors** to manage connected apps where controls are available. If you do not see a disconnect option, revoke access from the connected app's own settings and contact support if you still need help.

    Removing connector access does not delete content inside the connected app.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What can people see when I share a chat?">
    When you make a chat public, anyone with the share link can view the shared thread content included in the public share view. They do not get access to your account, settings, billing, connectors, or memory controls.

    Review the thread before sharing it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I delete files, media, chats, or translations?">
    You can delete supported Library items such as chats, saved media, and translations. Deleting a saved media item removes it from your Library. Deleting a chat removes the chat from your saved thread list.

    Some deletion actions cannot be undone.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where do I get account or deletion help?">
    In **Settings > Account**, you can update account details, request password help, or log out. For account deletion or data you cannot remove yourself, contact support.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Before contacting support

Include:

- Your account email.
- The page or feature involved.
- What you expected to happen.
- What happened instead.
- Approximate date, time, and timezone.
- Browser, device, and operating system.
- Your plan or team role if it matters.
- Error text or screenshots with sensitive details hidden.
